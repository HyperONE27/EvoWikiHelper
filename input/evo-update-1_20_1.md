## General Changes
***

- Concussive weapon damage ratio vs Shields: 100% -> {{nerf}}87.5%{{/nerf}}
- Explosive weapon damage ratio vs Shields: 100% -> {{nerf}}87.5%{{/nerf}}
<br>

Between Patches 1.19.1 and 1.20, we increased the Concussive and Explosive weapon damage ratio vs Shields from 75% to 100%. While we expected that this change could have significant implications across all three races, especially for the Brood War Terran versus StarCraft II Protoss matchup, preliminary playtesting suggests that this impact is greater than we are willing to accept in the context of mech play. To ensure that StarCraft II Protoss has enough options to deal with Brood War Terran mech play, we are reducing the damage ratio from 100% to 87.5%.

## Protoss Changes
***

{{unit "darktemplar"}}
- Increased build time: 25 -> {{nerf}}27.1{{/nerf}} [35 -> {{nerf}}38{{/nerf}}]
{{/unit}}

Until now, we have wanted to buff Zealot and Dragoon build times to match the throughput of production for other races. However, since we cannot do this without upsetting the balance of the early game against StarCraft II Terran and Protoss, we have compensated by making High Templar and Dark Templar — which are not typically present in the early game — produce more quickly.

However, it has become apparent that we underestimated the viability of Dark Templar against StarCraft II Terran. The Brood War Protoss vs StarCraft II Terran matchup is already known for the initative Protoss has to choose either the role of attacker or defender in the early game, and considering the viable scouting opportunities and openers available to Terran, we think that the threat of Dark Templar is one that could stand to be slightly reduced.

As such, we are increasing the build time of the Dark Templar.

***
{{unit "darkarchon"}}
- Decreased Mind Control cast range: 10 -> {{nerf}}9{{/nerf}}
{{/unit}}

Since Mind Control has the drawback of draining the Dark Archon's Plasma Shields and was so seldom used before with its pre-Patch 1.20 research and Energy costs, in addition to it being tied to a ground unit — unlike the Brood War Zerg Queen's Spawn Broodlings ability — we maintained its cast range at 10 as compensation.

However, with Mind Control buffed to its current Energy cost of 125, we think it is more important than ever to ensure that opponents have ample opportunity to prevent it from being cast against them, whether through units with longer range, or through EMP Round or Feedback. Therefore, we are reducing Mind Control's cast range to 9.
